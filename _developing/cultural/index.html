Highlighted specimens from other European museums

Crab spider

Photo of a female Crab spider, Thomisus onustus (Thomisidae). Crab spiders are characterized by the shape of their body and their capacity to walk laterally like crabs. Males of this species are many times smaller than females and have different coloration.
source: Natural History Museum of Crete

Urvogel Archaeopteryx lithographica

Origin of ancient bird Archaeopteryx lithographica, Eichstätt specimen
source: Jura Museum Eichstätt

Robberfly

Most of the fly specimens of the HNHM perished in a fire in 1956. Having been exhibited for the public, these large (5-6 cm) and impressive exotic robberflies luckily survived.
source: Natural History Museum of Hungary

Quartz (agate)

Cut nodule of agate (variety of mineral quartz) with numerous nice ball-shaped spherulites along the outer yellow calcite layer. The inner layer of geode is made up by clear macrocrystals of quartz.
